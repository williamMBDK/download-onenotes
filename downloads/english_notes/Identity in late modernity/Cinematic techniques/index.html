Cinematic techniques

Cinematic technique Characteristics
Framing

Close-up:The character(s) are framed very closely.

Long shot: Focus is placed on setting.

Medium shot: Focus is on more than one character.
Angles

Low angle: The character in question seems powerful and big.

High angle: The character in question seems small and maybe weak.

Eye level: Audience at the same level as characters.
Composition

Foreground.

Middle-ground.

Background.
Sound

Diegetic sound: Sound which is part of the film.

Non-diegetic sound: Sound which is not part of the film.

Contrapuntal sound: Sound being in direct opposition to the images of the film.
Lighting

Low key: Difficult to see everything in the image.

High key: Able to see everything in the image.
Colour Each colour has both a positive and negative symbolism.

GUIDE TO FILM ANALYSIS

Determine the type of film.

Analyse setting, characters, narrator and theme.

Use a shot by shot analysis of a scene to supplement your analysis e.g. your character analysis. This is where you use cinematic techniques such as framing, angle, composition.

Determine the type of sound used in the film. When does the filmmaker make use of diegetic, non-diegetic or contrapuntal sound and which effect does it have ?

Determine the type of lighting and colour used. When does the filmmaker make use of the different types of lighting and colours and why?

Analyse the film by looking at the narrative structure and the three-act structure model.
